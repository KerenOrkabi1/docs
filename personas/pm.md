# Project Manager

## Role summary
### Project Manager (JP Version)
Deliver project outputs and manage by exception

Responsible for the day-to-day management of a project within the constraints defined by the Project Board. They are accountable for delivering the agreed products according to the business case, tolerances, and quality expectations within their delegated authority.

## Responsibilities
- Planning and Control | Develop and maintain project plans, manage stages and work packages, monitor progress against baselines, and forecast deviations.
- Product Delivery | Ensure agreed products are delivered and maintain focus on product descriptions and quality criteria.
- Risk and Issue Management | Identify and manage risks and issues and escalate when tolerances are exceeded.
- Goverance Support | Ensure compliance with PMO governance controls and support decision making with reliable information.
- Stakeholder Management | Maintain stakeholder engagement and manage expectations and dependencies.
- Team Leadership | Direct project teams, align towards delivery outcomes and resolve delivery friction.

## Objectives
- Deliver Agreed products | In alignment with agreed scope and quality expectations.
- Control performance within Tolerances | Prevent exceptions from occurring.
- Manage Uncertainty | Anticipate risks, resolve issues, and ensure deviations don't derail delivery.
- Enable Predictability | Provide reliable, data driven forecasts and reporting.
- Meet Stakeholder Expectations | Balance competing priorities whilst delivering the approved scope.
- Maintain continued business justification | Protect the viability of the business case.

## Pain points
- Uncertainty and Change | Balancing shifting requirements, scope creep, and late decision-making.
- Dependency Constraints | Delays outside of direct control and hidden interdependencies.
- Resource Limitations | Insufficient capacity, competing priorities and skill mismatches and capability issues.
- Governance Friction | Excessive reporting requirements, delays in approvals, and administrative burdens.
- Stakeholder Misalignment | Conflicting expectations, vague acceptance criteria, and political pressures.
- Forecasting Pressures | Challenges in predicting outcomes in uncertain environments, needing to explain variances, and managing optimism bias.
- Constant Firefighting | Immediate issues, long-term planning, and long-term planning and re-planning.

## Emotional context
- Expectation Management | The Sponsor and key stakeholders expect certainty, precision, and predictability which conflicts with projects that by the very nature are uncertain.
- Cognitive Overload | Continuous management or risks, plans, issues, dependencies, and stakeholder dynamics.
- Visibility and Scrutiny | Projects are delivered in environments where problems are highly visible and success is often assumed.
- Emotional Toll | The project manager must project confidence, control, and calmness even when navigating ambiguity and turbulent delivery.

## Decisions they make
- what needs attention now
- how to update status and forecast
- when to raise a change, risk, issue, or request
- when to escalate because tolerances are exceeded
- which follow-up action can remove delivery friction fastest

## Data they need to see
- current delivery state
- overdue and at-risk items
- blockers and dependencies
- stale updates or missing progress signals
- forecast and milestone risk
- approvals and decisions waiting on others

## Default action focus
Delivery control and intervention across owned projects or within a single project context.

## KPI emphasis
- schedule confidence
- milestone health
- progress vs baseline
- blocker resolution
- forecast variance
- action aging / stale status

## AI support needs
- explain what changed
- surface what needs intervention first
- suggest lightweight follow-up actions
- summarize risk patterns
- support operational follow-up without taking control

## Permissions / action scope
- request changes or support
- update status
- mark reviewed or complete where permitted
- nudge or follow up
- open full records for deeper action
- does not act as portfolio-level approver by default

## How this persona changes Overview-family behavior
- buckets should center on delivery intervention urgency
- summary should prioritize delivery state over broader portfolio context
- AI should focus on actionability, blockers, and forecast risk
- detail can be narrower and more project specific
