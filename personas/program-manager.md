# Program Manager

## Role summary
Ensure continued business justification at scale

Accountable for the coordinated management of a group of related projects designed to deliver a measurable outcome. The program manager works to ensure projects collectively align with organisational strategy and deliver tangible benefits.

## Responsibilities
- Governance and Control | Ensure alignment with corporate strategy and oversee program-level risks, issues, and other RAID information.
- Strategic Alignment and Benefits Realisation | Ensure projects remain aligned to business needs and define benefits realisation for all projects.
- Coordination and Dependency Management | Manage inter-project dependencies and resolve resource conflicts.
- Stakeholder Management | Communicate program progress to key stakeholders.
- Financial Management | Monitor program budgets and forecasts.

## Objectives
- Deliver Strategic outcomes | Ensure initiatives collectively achieve organisation goals.
- Benefits Realisation | Ensure projects realise measurable benefits.
- Maintain governance and control | Provide predictability, transparency, and accountability.
- Optimise investment decisions | By balanceing cost, risk, and value.
- Reduce delivery friction | Resolve conflicts, dependencies, and bottle-necks.
- Enable executive confidence | Provide reliable data for decision-making.

## Pain points
- Strategic Ambiguity | Misalignment between strategy and operations.
- Dependency Complexity | Hidden technical or organisational dependencies.
- Resource Constraints | Competing resource demands across projects.
- Stakeholder Misalignment | Conflicting agendas and political tensions.
- Benefits Realisation challenges | Difficulty measuring benefits and value delivered.
- Constant re-prioritisation | Reactive decision cycles, executive interventions and scope volatility.

## Emotional context
- Persistent Cognitive Load | Managing multiple projects, continuous decision-making, and high mental fatigue.
- Accountability without full control | Responsible for outcomes which are influenced by multiple often conflicted stakeholders.
- Expectation Tension | Executives expect certainty from a delivery environment which has inherent uncertainty.
- High Stress | Regularly mediating disagreements and balancing organisational politics.
- Visibility & Scrutiny | Any failure is highly visible and success is perceived as an expectation by key stakeholders.
- Emotional Toll | Maintaining composure whilst absorbing stakeholder anxiety and projecting confidence during delivery turbulence.
- Internal Friction | Caught between strategic requirements and delivery reality often compounded by recurring system issues.

## Decisions they make
- what needs escalation or intervention at program level
- which trade-offs are acceptable across projects
- whether to approve, challenge, or defer requests
- where to focus limited capacity and funding attention
- when to drill deeper before making a decision

## Data they need to see
- program health
- project drift and dependency impact
- approvals and requests needing review
- financial state and forecast pressure
- benefit alignment and governance signals
- bottlenecks affecting multiple projects

## Default action focus
Program-level control, exception management, dependency resolution, approval review, and financial oversight.

## KPI emphasis
- aggregate program health
- cross-project risk
- dependency pressure
- approval backlog
- budget vs forecast at program level
- benefit and delivery confidence

## AI support needs
- prioritize what needs review first
- explain why an item matters at program level
- summarize cross-project impact
- suggest plan B actions or trade-off options
- support human-led approval and intervention decisions

## Permissions / action scope
- may approve, challenge, escalate, or redirect requests depending on org model
- may re-prioritize or intervene across related projects
- usually has broader visibility than a single PM

## How this persona changes Overview-family behavior
- overview should promote cross-project state and executive-summary visibility
- buckets should reflect intervention categories, not task lists
- AI should emphasize trade-offs, program impact, and prioritization
- actionability should support decision review, not just row-level updates
