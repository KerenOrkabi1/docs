# Portfolio Manager

## Role summary
Govern investment decisions

Accountable for selecting, prioritising, governing, and optimising and organisations total investment in projects and programmes to ensure alignment with strategic objectives whilst balancing risk appetite and resource capacity.

## Responsibilities
- Strategic Investment Governance | Align with organisational strategy, maintain business justification, and advise executives on investment 'trade-offs'.
- Prioritisation and Selection | Evaluate proposals, optimise sequencing of initiatives and resolve competing demands.
- Value and Benefits | Ensure benefits alignment with strategy, monitor portfolio value delivery, and stop low value under performing initiatives.
- Portfolio Risk | Balance short and long term investments, innovation vs operational stability, and assess aggregate risk exposure.
- Resource Optimisation | Identify bottlenecks and constraints, support cross-program allocations, and align demand with organisational capacity.
- Performance Monitoring | Provide executive reporting and insights and ensure transparency of investment performance.

## Objectives
- Ensure Strategic Alignment | Fund initiatives that advance organisational strategic goals.
- Optimise Investment Value | Maximise return on investment.
- Maintain business justification | Ensure initiatives remain viable.
- Balance Risk and Opportunity | Avoid only focussing on the exposure of failure.
- Enable executive decision-making | Provide clarity to key stakeholders regarding complex delivery.
- Control Change Saturation | Prevent organisational overload by effectively sequencing initiatives for maximum impact.

## Pain points
- Strategic Ambiguity | Lack of clarity in executive priorities and strategic narratives including rapid shifts in direction.
- Demand vs Capacity Conflicts | Too many initiatives chasing scarce resources with a pressure to approve everything and overcommit.
- Stakeholder Pressure | Competing executive agendas and emotionally charged prioritisation battles.
- Benefits Uncertainty | Inflated benefit forecasts, poor ownership, and difficulties in measuring tangible value.
- Realignment Resistance | Organisational reluctance to stop failing initiatives compounded by a sunk cost bias.

## Emotional context
- Allocation Decisions | Decisions feel consequential and political.
- Expectation Tension | Executives want certainty, predictability, and fast results which conflict with the inherent certainty in portfolio environments.
- Political Fatigue | Regular exposure to hidden agendas, escalations, & conflicts.
- Cognitive Overload | Continuous Balancing of value vs risk, innovation vs stability, and demand vs capacity.
- Emotional Toll | Portfolio manager must convey objectivity, confidence, and rationality whjilst absorbing organisational tension.
- Internal Friction | Frustration with unrealistice demand, constant reprioritisation fatigue, and pressure to justify unpopular decisions.

## Decisions they make
- which initiatives deserve investment attention
- where to prioritize, defer, or stop work
- how to balance aggregate risk and value
- when to reallocate or hold funding
- which items need executive escalation or confidence-building clarity

## Data they need to see
- portfolio state and investment health
- aggregate risk and performance signals
- value and benefits alignment
- funding/envelope position
- forecast pressure and cross-program impact
- executive-level transparency of what needs attention

## Default action focus
Strategic oversight, prioritisation, financial governance, and investment-level decision support.

## KPI emphasis
- portfolio value alignment
- investment health
- aggregate risk exposure
- capacity pressure
- budget/envelope position
- executive decision confidence

## AI support needs
- summarize portfolio-level state clearly
- identify the biggest decisions needing attention
- highlight trade-offs and consequences
- support what-if thinking and option framing
- provide confidence-building narrative without hiding uncertainty

## Permissions / action scope
- typically broadest approval and governance visibility among the three personas in scope
- may approve, stop, rebalance, or escalate depending on organisation model
